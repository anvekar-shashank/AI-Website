Research and Publications

The Department of Electronics and Communication Engineering actively promotes research and innovation as a core component of academic and professional growth. Faculty members and students are engaged in research across diverse domains such as VLSI design, embedded systems, wireless communication, signal and image processing, IoT, artificial intelligence, machine learning, robotics, and renewable energy systems.

Research activities are supported through dedicated project laboratories, access to advanced software tools, collaborations with industries and research organizations, and participation in funded projects. The department encourages students to take up mini-projects, final-year projects, and interdisciplinary research that address real-world challenges and societal needs.

Faculty and students regularly publish research articles in reputed journals and conferences, present papers, and contribute to patents and innovations. By fostering a culture of inquiry and creativity, the department aims to develop cutting-edge solutions, strengthen industry–academia linkages, and contribute to the advancement of knowledge in electronics and communication engineering.

Research Profile: Dr. Praveen J is a prolific researcher with an exceptional publication record spanning over a decade. His research expertise encompasses VLSI design, low-power circuit design, BIST (Built-In Self-Test) architectures, antenna design, and emerging technologies in nanotechnology and IoT. With 81 publications including 11 Scopus-indexed journals, his work demonstrates significant contributions to electronics and communication engineering. His research on adiabatic logic circuits, FinFET technology, fractal antennas, and VLSI architectures has been published in high-impact international journals. He has also filed 4 patents, including one granted patent on stroke detection using VLSI architecture, showcasing his commitment to translating research into practical innovations. His collaborative work with students has resulted in numerous conference presentations and journal publications, reflecting his dedication to mentoring and advancing the field.
