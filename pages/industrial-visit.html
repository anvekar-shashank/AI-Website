Industrial Visits Program

Industrial visits provide Electronics and Communication Engineering students with valuable exposure to real-world industrial environments, bridging the gap between academic knowledge and practical applications. By observing live processes, advanced technologies, and modern equipment in industries such as telecommunications, semiconductor manufacturing, embedded systems, automation, broadcasting, and electronics product development, students gain a deeper understanding of how theoretical concepts are applied in practice.

These visits help students relate classroom learning to industry practices, enhance their technical awareness, and develop insights into current trends, challenges, and innovations in the field of electronics and communication. Industrial visits also promote professional interaction with industry experts, instill teamwork and discipline, and motivate students toward research, innovation, employability, and entrepreneurship.

Industrial Visit: LAHARI Advanced Electronics Test Facility

Our industrial visit to LAHARI Advanced Electronics Test Facility gave us practical exposure to how advanced electronics are tested and validated to meet industry standards. We learned about real-world testing processes, quality assurance, and the importance of reliability in electronic products. The visit helped bridge the gap between theoretical knowledge and industrial practices, and gave us insight into career opportunities in the ESDM sector.
