Academic Programs

The Department of Electronics and Communication Engineering offers comprehensive programs designed to prepare students for successful careers in the rapidly evolving field of electronics and communication technology.

B.Tech. in Electronics and Communication Engineering

Duration: 4 Years (8 Semesters)

Eligibility: 10+2 with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics

Program Overview:

Our flagship undergraduate program provides comprehensive training in electronics and communication engineering. The curriculum is designed to balance theoretical knowledge with practical skills, preparing graduates for diverse career opportunities in industry and research.

Key Features:

  • Industry-aligned curriculum with regular updates
  • State-of-the-art laboratory facilities
  • Mandatory internships and industry projects
  • Research opportunities from second year onwards
  • International exchange and collaboration programs

Specialization Tracks:

  • VLSI Design and Embedded Systems
  • Communication Systems and Networks
  • Signal and Image Processing
  • IoT and Automation
  • Robotics and AI in Electronics

Co-Curricular Programs

  • Certification Courses: Industry-recognized certifications in embedded systems, IoT, VLSI tools
  • Workshop Series: Regular hands-on workshops on emerging technologies
  • Hackathons: Annual Ignitron hackathon and participation in national-level competitions
  • Technical Clubs: Student-run clubs for robotics, electronics, coding, and innovation
  • Skill Development: Soft skills, communication, and leadership training

Career Pathways

Our graduates pursue diverse career paths including:

  • VLSI Design Engineer
  • Embedded Systems Developer
  • Communication Systems Engineer
  • IoT Solutions Architect
  • Signal Processing Specialist
  • Electronics Hardware Engineer
  • Research and Development
  • Higher Studies (M.Tech./Ph.D.)
  • Entrepreneurship and Startups
